## Background

N-Acetyl Selank Amidate is a modified version of selank (Thr-Lys-Pro-Arg-Pro-Gly-Pro), the Russian-approved anxiolytic heptapeptide derived from the tuftsin sequence. N-terminal acetylation and C-terminal amidation protect the peptide from proteolytic breakdown, theoretically extending its duration of action and potency.

## Relationship to Selank

Standard selank is registered in Russia for anxiety and asthenic conditions. The modified amidate form is not part of Russian pharmacopoeia and has no independent regulatory or clinical trial history. It exists only in the research chemical market.

## Proposed Mechanism

Selank's mechanism involves modulation of GABAergic transmission and enkephalin degradation inhibition (via MetEnk stabilisation). It also modulates BDNF expression, IL-6, and T-helper lymphocyte balance. The amidate modification is assumed to amplify these effects by extending bioavailability at intranasal receptor sites.

## Community Use

Online nootropic communities report NA-Selank Amidate as producing anxiolysis without sedation, improved verbal fluency, reduced social anxiety, and immune modulation. These reports are anecdotal. No human pharmacokinetic or pharmacodynamic studies have been published for this specific variant.
