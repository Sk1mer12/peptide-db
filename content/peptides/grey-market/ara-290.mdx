## Design Rationale

Erythropoietin (EPO) was recognised to have tissue-protective and anti-inflammatory effects beyond its haematopoietic function. However, full-length EPO stimulates red blood cell production and increases thromboembolic risk when used for tissue protection. ARA-290 was designed by mapping the region of EPO that engages the innate repair receptor (IRR) — a heterodimer of the EPO receptor and the beta-common (βc) receptor — rather than the classical homodimeric EPO receptor that drives erythropoiesis.

The result is a short cyclic peptide (sequence based on helix B residues of EPO, approximately Ile-Gln-Glu-Ala-His-Ile-Asn-His-Phe-Tyr-Lys with a cyclising structure) that activates the IRR without any erythropoietic activity. This dissociation is the key innovation.

## Clinical Evidence

The most advanced data come from studies in sarcoidosis patients with small-fibre neuropathy (SFN) — a painful condition caused by loss of unmyelinated C-fibres and small myelinated Aδ fibres. In a randomised, placebo-controlled Phase 2 trial (Niesters et al., 2013), ARA-290 significantly reduced neuropathic pain scores and improved corneal nerve fibre density at 28 days. A follow-up study by Brines et al. (2014) confirmed nerve regeneration in the same population.

## Status

No phase 3 trial has been completed and no NDA has been submitted. Araim Pharmaceuticals has conducted the principal research. The compound is available from speciality research peptide vendors and is occasionally referenced in online chronic pain and sarcoidosis communities.
