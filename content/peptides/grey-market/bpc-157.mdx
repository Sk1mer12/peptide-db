## Research Background

BPC-157 is a pentadecapeptide (15 amino acids) originally isolated from human gastric juice. Preclinical research, primarily from groups at the University of Zagreb, has reported accelerated healing of tendons, ligaments, muscle, and intestinal tissue in rat models. Effects on nitric oxide synthesis and angiogenesis have been proposed as mechanisms.

## Human Evidence Status

There are no completed randomised controlled trials in humans demonstrating safety or efficacy. All available data is from animal (predominantly rodent) models. The translation of rodent healing research to human outcomes is unknown.

## Regulatory Status

FDA explicitly declined to include BPC-157 in compounding formulations (Category 2 decision). It is not a substance with established drug status. Grey-market vendors sell it as a "research chemical" or "not for human use," but reports of widespread human use for injury recovery and gut conditions are extensively documented in online communities.
