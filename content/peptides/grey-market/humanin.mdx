## Background

Humanin was discovered in 2001 by Nishimoto et al. as a peptide derived from the 16S rRNA region of mitochondrial DNA. It exerts anti-apoptotic effects in neurons (originally identified in Alzheimer's disease models) and has subsequently been found to improve insulin sensitivity and protect against cardiovascular stress in animal models.

## Longevity Associations

Higher circulating humanin levels have been detected in centenarians and their offspring vs. age-matched controls. IGF-1 receptor binding by humanin may relate to conserved longevity pathways. No clinical trials of exogenous humanin in humans have been completed.
