## Background

DSIP (Trp-Ala-Gly-Gly-Asp-Ala-Ser-Gly-Glu) was isolated in 1977 by Monnier et al. from the venous blood of sleeping rabbits. Despite initial excitement about its potential as a sleep-promoting agent, subsequent research produced highly inconsistent results regarding its sleep-modulating effects. Some studies found effects on opiate withdrawal and stress. Its mechanism of action remains poorly defined and no validated receptor has been identified.
