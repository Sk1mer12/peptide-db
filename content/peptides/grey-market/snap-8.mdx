## Background

SNAP-8 (INCI: Acetyl Glutamyl Heptapeptide-3) is a synthetic octapeptide developed by Lipotec (Lubrizol Life Science) as a next-generation evolution of Argireline (Acetyl Hexapeptide-3). Its sequence is Ac-Glu-Glu-Met-Gln-Arg-Arg-Ala-NH2, extending the Argireline sequence by one C-terminal alanine residue. This modification is claimed to improve binding affinity to the SNARE complex target relative to the parent hexapeptide.

## Mechanism

Like Argireline, SNAP-8 acts by mimicking the N-terminal domain of SNAP-25 (synaptosomal-associated protein 25), a component of the neuronal SNARE complex required for acetylcholine vesicle exocytosis at the neuromuscular junction. Competitive binding of SNAP-8 to the SNARE assembly site attenuates neurotransmitter release, reducing muscle contractility at the site of topical application. The additional alanine is proposed to improve steric complementarity with the SNARE binding groove.

## Efficacy Claims vs Evidence

Lipotec's internal data claim that SNAP-8 produces a statistically significant reduction in the depth of wrinkles associated with facial muscle contraction (expression wrinkles — periorbital and forehead) at 4–8% concentration in topical formulations after 28 days. No independent peer-reviewed RCT has been published for SNAP-8 specifically. Efficacy evidence is largely extrapolated from the Argireline literature and Lipotec proprietary studies.

## Cosmetic Market Position

SNAP-8 is positioned in premium peptide serums as a more potent alternative to Argireline. It is an INCI-listed ingredient and legally used worldwide as a cosmetic active. Several branded skincare products include SNAP-8 in combination with other signal peptides (Matrixyl, Leuphasyl).

## Injectable Variants

Grey-market peptide vendors occasionally sell SNAP-8 as an injectable compound. No safety or pharmacokinetic data exist for injectable use; this is entirely experimental and carries standard injection-related risks.
