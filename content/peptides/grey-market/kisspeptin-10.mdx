## Background

Kisspeptin neurons in the arcuate nucleus and AVPV are the primary regulators of GnRH pulsatile release. Kisspeptin-10 is the minimal active fragment of kisspeptin (KISS1 gene product) that fully activates GPR54. It has been studied in controlled research trials for triggering ovulation in IVF (as an alternative to hCG), anorexia nervosa, and hypogonadotropic hypogonadism.

## Research Interest

Several pharmaceutical companies are developing long-acting kisspeptin analogues for endocrine disorders. The short-acting Kp-10 is used as a research tool but has no approved therapeutic formulation.
