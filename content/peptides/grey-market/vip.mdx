## Background

VIP is a 28-amino acid neuropeptide belonging to the secretin/glucagon superfamily, endogenously produced in neurons of the central and peripheral nervous system, gut, and immune cells. It is a pleiotropic mediator with potent anti-inflammatory, immunomodulatory, vasodilatory, and bronchodilatory activities.

## Mechanism

VIP signals through VPAC1 and VPAC2 receptors (GPCRs), increasing intracellular cAMP. Key downstream effects include: inhibition of pro-inflammatory cytokine production (TNF-α, IL-6, IL-12), promotion of T-regulatory cell differentiation, inhibition of mast cell degranulation, relaxation of airway and vascular smooth muscle, and modulation of circadian rhythm and neuroendocrine function.

## Grey-Market Use

VIP has gained significant traction in integrative/functional medicine practices for:
- **CIRS (Chronic Inflammatory Response Syndrome)** — part of the Shoemaker protocol; intranasal VIP claimed to reduce neuroinflammation and restore regulatory balance
- **Mast cell activation syndrome (MCAS)** — anti-inflammatory and mast cell-stabilising properties
- **Long COVID** — exploratory use for post-infectious dysautonomia and inflammatory sequelae

## Evidence Status

No completed Phase 3 RCTs for grey-market indications. Aviptadil (synthetic VIP) has been investigated for ARDS and COVID-19 but did not achieve primary endpoints in major trials. CIRS protocol data are observational, not controlled.
