## Background

Selank (Thr-Lys-Pro-Arg-Pro-Gly-Pro) was developed at the Institute of Molecular Genetics of the Russian Academy of Sciences. It combines the sequence of tuftsin with a Pro-Gly-Pro extension for stability. Russian clinical studies report anxiolytic effects comparable to benzodiazepines without dependence or sedation, along with nootropic and immune-modulatory properties.

## Evidence Quality

The evidence base consists almost entirely of Russian-language publications from institutions associated with the developer. Independent replication in Western peer-reviewed journals is absent. The compound is not classifiable as evidence-based medicine by Western regulatory standards.
