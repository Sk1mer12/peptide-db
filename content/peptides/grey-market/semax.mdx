## Background

Semax (Met-Glu-His-Phe-Pro-Gly-Pro) is a synthetic ACTH analogue derived from the 4-10 fragment of ACTH. It enhances brain-derived neurotrophic factor (BDNF) expression, protects against ischaemia-induced neuronal death, and reportedly improves attention, learning, and working memory in rodent models. It is registered in Russia for ischaemic stroke recovery, brain trauma, and optic nerve disease.

## Evidence Status

Clinical research base is primarily from Russian institutions. No Phase 2 or 3 RCTs have been published in Western peer-reviewed journals. Available in the US as an unregulated research chemical.
