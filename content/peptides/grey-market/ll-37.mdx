## Background

LL-37 is the C-terminal cleavage product of the human cathelicidin precursor hCAP18, produced by neutrophils, epithelial cells, and keratinocytes. It disrupts bacterial membranes, neutralises LPS, promotes wound healing, and modulates adaptive immunity. It is the only known human cathelicidin.

## Drug Development Interest

LL-37 analogues are in early clinical development for chronic wounds and lung infections (e.g., in cystic fibrosis). Full-length LL-37 itself has not entered Phase 2 trials as a drug due to its stability challenges and dose-dependent cytotoxicity.
