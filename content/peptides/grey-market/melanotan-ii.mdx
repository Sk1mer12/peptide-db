## Background

Melanotan II was developed at the University of Arizona as a synthetic melanocortin peptide to study UV-independent tanning. It activates MC1R (skin pigmentation), MC3R, MC4R (sexual arousal, appetite, autonomic), and MC5R. Development was abandoned due to adverse event profile. Its analogue Melanotan I (afamelanotide) was refined and approved as Scenesse for erythropoietic protoporphyria.

## Safety Warning

Multiple health regulatory agencies (FDA, MHRA UK, TGA Australia) have issued explicit warnings against using Melanotan II due to serious adverse events including abnormal mole changes, cardiovascular events, and a documented melanoma case temporally linked to use. It is sold illegally online despite these warnings.
