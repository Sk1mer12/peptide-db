## Background

Sermorelin acetate (GRF 1-29 NH2) was FDA-approved as Geref Diagnostic for testing pituitary function and as Geref for paediatric GH deficiency. Serono withdrew Geref from the US market in 2008 for commercial reasons, not due to safety. Since then, compounding pharmacies have continued to compound sermorelin acetate, marketed heavily through anti-aging medicine.

## Current Status

FDA has not explicitly banned compounded sermorelin, but ongoing review of GHRH analogues and growth hormone secretagogues has created regulatory uncertainty. Anti-aging clinics frequently combine it with GHRP peptides (often called "sermorelin/ipamorelin" blends).
