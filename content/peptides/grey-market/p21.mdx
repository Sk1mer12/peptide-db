## Background

P21 (also written P021) is a tetrapeptide derived from the neurotrophin-binding domain of CNTF (Ciliary Neurotrophic Factor). It was reported by researchers at the Rashid Laboratory (Aga Khan University) to reduce tau phosphorylation and amyloid burden, promote neurogenesis, and improve cognitive function in rodent Alzheimer's models. It acts by binding gp130 and activating JAK/STAT3 signalling without triggering the full inflammatory cascade associated with full-length CNTF.

## Status

Very limited research base; primarily from one group. No human trials. Sold by research chemical vendors with no quality standards or characterisation requirements.
