## Background

Epitalon is derived from research conducted primarily by Vladimir Khavinson at the St. Petersburg Institute of Bioregulation and Gerontology. Animal studies claimed lifespan extension and telomere elongation. The research base consists largely of Russian-language publications that have not been independently replicated in peer-reviewed Western journals.

## Evidence Assessment

No randomised, placebo-controlled, peer-reviewed human trials exist. The claim that epitalon extends human lifespan via telomerase activation is not substantiated by clinical evidence. Telomerase activation itself is a double-edged phenomenon — while potentially anti-aging, it is also a hallmark of cancer cell immortalisation.

## Status

Not approved anywhere. No IND or CTA filed with FDA or EMA.
