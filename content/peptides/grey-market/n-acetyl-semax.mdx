## Background

N-Acetyl Semax Amidate is a chemically modified form of semax (ACTH(4-7)PGP) in which the N-terminal methionine is acetylated and the C-terminal proline is amidated. These modifications protect the peptide from aminopeptidase and carboxypeptidase degradation, extending its duration of action compared to standard semax.

## Relationship to Semax

Standard semax (Met-Glu-His-Phe-Pro-Gly-Pro) is approved in Russia for cerebrovascular disorders. The acetylated/amidated variant is not part of the Russian regulatory framework and has no independent clinical trial history. It is sold exclusively in the research chemical / nootropic market, with claimed enhanced potency based on structural analogy with other modified peptides.

## Proposed Mechanism

Like semax, N-Acetyl Semax Amidate is proposed to act through:
- Upregulation of BDNF (brain-derived neurotrophic factor) expression
- Modulation of dopamine and serotonin neurotransmission
- Reduction of neuroinflammation and oxidative stress
- ACE inhibitory activity via the Pro-Gly-Pro tail

## Evidence

No controlled human clinical trials have been conducted for this specific variant. Evidence is extrapolated from the parent semax literature (primarily Russian-language animal studies and small clinical reports). User-reported effects in online communities include improved focus, memory consolidation, and mood stabilisation.
