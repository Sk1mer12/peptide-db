## Background

IGF-1 LR3 was developed as a research tool to study IGF-1 signalling without the confounding effects of IGFBP binding. The 13-amino acid N-terminal extension prevents IGFBP-3 binding, and the Arg3 substitution reduces affinity for IGFBP-1, resulting in dramatically higher bioavailability vs. native IGF-1. It is used in cell culture and animal research.

## Human Use Context

IGF-1 LR3 is widely used in bodybuilding and athletic performance contexts for its anabolic effects. This use is not approved, not studied in humans, and poses significant risks including hypoglycaemia and long-term oncogenic potential through IGF-1R signalling.
