## Background and Distinction from Related Compounds

Modified GRF (1-29) is frequently — and incorrectly — marketed as "CJC-1295 without DAC." True CJC-1295 (DAC:GRF) incorporates a maleimidopropionamide Drug Affinity Complex (DAC) at the C-terminus that covalently binds to serum albumin, extending half-life to approximately 7 days. Mod GRF (1-29) carries no DAC and has a half-life of ~30 minutes, making it functionally a pulsatile GH stimulator rather than a continuous one. Sermorelin (GHRH 1-29 unmodified) has a half-life under 10 minutes; the four substitutions in Mod GRF (D-Ala at position 2, Ala at position 8, Ala at position 15, Leu at position 27) confer dipeptidyl peptidase IV resistance without extending half-life to multi-day durations.

## Mechanism

Mod GRF (1-29) binds and activates the GHRH receptor (GHRHR) on pituitary somatotrophs, stimulating pulsatile GH release. When combined with a GHRP (e.g., Ipamorelin, GHRP-2), synergistic GH release is observed, as GHRPs act on the ghrelin/GHS-R1a pathway and also suppress endogenous somatostatin tone.

## Evidence and Clinical Status

No completed phase 1–3 clinical trials exist for this specific compound. The pharmacology is extrapolated from the approved Sermorelin compound (GHRH 1-29) and from CJC-1295 phase 2 data. All current human use is off-label, anecdotally documented in online communities focused on body composition and anti-aging protocols.

## Regulatory and Doping Status

Not FDA-approved. WADA prohibits all GHRH analogues in and out of competition under the S2 category. FDA has signalled that GHRH analogues are not suitable for compounding.
