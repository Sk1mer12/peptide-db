## Distinction from FS-344

Follistatin-315 is generated from the FST gene through alternative splicing and proteolytic processing, resulting in a 315-amino acid isoform that lacks the 27-amino acid EI domain (heparin-binding C-terminal extension) present in FS-344. This structural difference has profound pharmacokinetic consequences: without the heparin-binding domain, FS-315 does not associate strongly with cell surface proteoglycans and distributes more broadly in the systemic circulation.

## Binding and Systemic Activity

FS-315 binds activin A, myostatin, and GDF-11 with high affinity and neutralises them in a non-reversible complex. Because of its systemic distribution, FS-315 can inhibit activin signalling throughout the body — including at the hypothalamic-pituitary axis, where activin regulates FSH secretion. In animal studies, systemic FS-315 administration causes ovarian cysts, infertility, and pituitary disruption — effects not observed with locally acting FS-344.

## Research Applications

FS-315 is used in research settings where systemic myostatin/activin blockade is the goal: fibrosis models, wasting disease models, and studies of GDF-11 in aging. In rodent muscle wasting models (cachexia, sarcopenia), FS-315 has shown significant efficacy in restoring muscle mass.

## Safety Profile

The systemic reproductive effects observed in FS-315 animal studies represent a significant safety concern for human use. These distinguish it from FS-344 and make FS-315 the higher-risk isoform despite comparable anabolic potency.
