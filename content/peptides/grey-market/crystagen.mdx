## Background

Crystagen is a short peptide bioregulator from Vladimir Khavinson's organ-specific peptide series, derived from retinal tissue fractions. Unlike the precisely characterised members of the series (e.g., Epitalon as AEDG, Vilon as KE, Testagen as KEDV), the exact sequence of Crystagen has not been consistently reported in indexed English-language literature — various vendor sources cite different sequences, reflecting the limited independent characterisation of this compound compared to others in the Khavinson series.

## Proposed Indications

The Khavinson group proposes Crystagen for use in retinal ageing conditions including age-related macular degeneration (AMD), diabetic retinopathy, and general photoreceptor senescence. The proposed mechanism follows the group's broader model: organ-specific peptide → TATA-box interaction → upregulation of tissue-repair genes in retinal cells.

## Evidence Limitations

Evidence is limited to Russian-language publications from the Khavinson group and affiliated institutes. No randomised controlled trials or phase 1 safety studies in any indexed English-language journal have been published. The route of administration (subconjunctival, topical drops) is unconventional for peptide delivery and has not been characterised pharmacokinetically in peer-reviewed literature.

## Vendor Availability Note

Crystagen is one of the less commonly stocked Khavinson peptides compared to Epitalon, Vilon, or Thymalin. Sequence purity and authenticity from grey-market vendors is unverified and particularly uncertain for this compound.
