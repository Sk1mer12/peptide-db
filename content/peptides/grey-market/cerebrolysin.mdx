## Background

Cerebrolysin is a standardised, low-molecular-weight peptide mixture prepared from porcine brain tissue via controlled enzymatic proteolysis. Approximately 25% of the preparation consists of biologically active peptide fragments (mainly under 10 kDa), with the remainder being free amino acids. It mimics neurotrophic factors such as NGF, BDNF, and IGF-1 without crossing the blood-brain barrier as intact proteins.

## Mechanism

The neuropeptide fraction stimulates acetylcholine synthesis, modulates AMPA and NMDA receptor function, reduces apoptosis via Bcl-2 upregulation, and promotes neuroplasticity via MAP kinase pathways. It is proposed to reduce excitotoxic neuronal death following ischaemia and to support synaptic remodelling in neurodegeneration.

## Evidence Base

The evidence is mixed but substantial in volume. Phase 3 trials (CASTA, 2012) in acute ischaemic stroke showed no significant benefit on the primary Barthel Index endpoint. A Cochrane review (2021) found modest improvement in neurological deficit scores with uncertain clinical significance. Trials in Alzheimer's disease show consistent but modest cognitive improvement vs placebo. The Soviet/Russian/Chinese evidence base is large but subject to replication and quality concerns.

## Regulatory Status

Approved as a prescription drug in Russia, China, South Korea, Ukraine, and several Eastern European countries. The Austrian manufacturer (EVER Neuro Pharma) markets it across Europe. The FDA has not approved it, citing insufficient evidence from adequately controlled trials conducted under US standards.
