## Background

MOTS-c was identified in 2015 by researchers at USC as a peptide encoded within the 12S rRNA gene of mitochondrial DNA. In rodent studies, MOTS-c administration improved insulin sensitivity, reduced obesity with a high-fat diet, and showed exercise-mimetic effects via AMPK activation. Circulating MOTS-c levels appear to decline with age in humans, making it a longevity research target.

## Human Evidence

Observational studies have found correlations between endogenous MOTS-c levels and metabolic health in humans. No interventional human trials have been published. WADA added MOTS-c to its prohibited list as a "peptide hormone, growth factor, related substance and mimetic."
