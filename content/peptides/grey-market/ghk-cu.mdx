## Background

GHK-Cu is found naturally in human plasma, saliva, and urine. It was originally characterised by Loren Pickart. It binds copper (II) with high affinity and is involved in wound healing, anti-inflammatory signalling, and matrix remodelling. As a cosmetic ingredient, it is legal and widely used in anti-aging skincare formulations.

## Injectable Use

The injectable use of GHK-Cu is not approved anywhere. Animal studies suggest systemic GHK-Cu promotes angiogenesis and tissue remodelling, but human pharmacokinetics, safety, and efficacy are uncharacterised. There are no clinical trials of injectable GHK-Cu in humans.
