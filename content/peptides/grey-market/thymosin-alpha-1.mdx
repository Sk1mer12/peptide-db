## Background

Thymosin alpha-1 is the N-terminal acetylated fragment of thymosin fraction 5, originally isolated by Allan Goldstein from thymosin preparations. It promotes maturation and differentiation of T lymphocytes and enhances NK cell and dendritic cell activity. It has been used clinically in Asia and Italy since the 1990s.

## Approved Uses (non-US)

- **Chronic hepatitis B**: immune adjuvant
- **Chronic hepatitis C**: in combination with interferons
- **Immune restoration**: post-chemotherapy or in immunocompromised patients
- **COVID-19**: used in critically ill patients in China during pandemic

## US Status

Not FDA-approved. No NDA has been filed by SciClone in the US. Available from grey-market research chemical vendors.
