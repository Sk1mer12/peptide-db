## Background

Matrixyl is the trade name for Palmitoyl Pentapeptide-4 (INCI: Palmitoyl Pentapeptide-4), a synthetic pentapeptide with the sequence Lys-Thr-Thr-Lys-Ser coupled to palmitic acid at the N-terminus. It was developed by Sederma (now part of Croda International) and launched in the early 2000s. The sequence KTTKS is derived from the pro-collagen I type alpha 2 chain and is a known matrikine — a matrix-derived peptide that signals extracellular matrix remodelling.

## Mechanism

Pal-KTTKS stimulates dermal fibroblasts to upregulate synthesis of:
- **Collagen I and IV** — structural integrity
- **Elastin** — skin elasticity
- **Fibronectin** — cell adhesion and matrix organisation
- **Glycosaminoglycans** — dermal hydration

The palmitoyl group improves skin penetration through the stratum corneum by increasing lipophilicity, enabling the peptide to reach dermal fibroblasts. Once in the dermis, KTTKS acts as a pro-collagen signal mimicking the feedback loop that naturally upregulates matrix production following collagen degradation.

## Clinical Evidence

Robinson et al. (2005) conducted a double-blind, split-face RCT comparing 3 ppm Pal-KTTKS vs vehicle twice daily for 12 weeks in subjects with mild-to-moderate facial wrinkles. Pal-KTTKS produced statistically significant improvements in wrinkle area and severity indices assessed by profilometry and expert grading. Effect sizes were modest but consistent.

Independent confirmatory studies show consistent, if small, collagen-stimulating effects in ex vivo human skin models.

## Matrixyl 3000

Matrixyl 3000 is a commercially formulated combination of Pal-KTTKS (Matrixyl) and Palmitoyl Tripeptide-1 (Pal-GHK), the latter of which synergistically promotes collagen and hyaluronic acid synthesis. The combination is used in luxury anti-aging skincare products.
