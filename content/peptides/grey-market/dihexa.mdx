## Background

Dihexa was developed by Joseph Harding and colleagues at Washington State University as a derivative of angiotensin IV research. In rodent studies, it dramatically improved performance in models of cognitive dysfunction (Alzheimer's, traumatic brain injury) and was reported to enhance synaptogenesis. The claim of 10⁷× greater potency than BDNF in vitro attracted significant attention.

## Evidence Status

All published evidence is from preclinical rodent studies from one research group. There are no human pharmacokinetic, safety, or efficacy data. No IND application has been filed. It is available from research chemical vendors.
