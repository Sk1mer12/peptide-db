## Background

FOXO4-DRI was reported by Jan van Deursen's group at Mayo Clinic (2017, Cell) as a peptide that disrupts the interaction between FOXO4 and p53 in senescent cells, allowing p53 to activate apoptosis in those cells. In aged mice, it reportedly restored physical fitness and hair density and improved kidney function.

## Significant Concerns

Senescent cells have context-dependent functions — they contribute to wound healing, tumour suppression, and embryonic development. Non-selective elimination of senescent cells carries risks that are not well characterised. p53 pathway interference has inherent cancer-risk implications. This compound is considered highly experimental even within the longevity research community.
