## Background

Hexarelin (His-D-2-MeTrp-Ala-Trp-D-Phe-Lys-NH2) is one of the most potent synthetic GH secretagogues, producing GH pulses comparable to GHRP-6. It additionally activates the CD36 scavenger receptor (a non-ghrelin pathway) in cardiac tissue, producing cardioprotective effects in rodent ischaemia models. This dual mechanism has generated research interest in cardiac protection independent of GH.

## Status

Reached Phase 2 clinical trials for GH deficiency in Europe (Mediolanum Farmaceutici) in the 1990s–2000s. Development was not continued commercially. Available as a research chemical.
