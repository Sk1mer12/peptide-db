## Background

MGF (IGF-1Ec) is produced in skeletal muscle in response to mechanical loading (exercise, injury). It acts locally on satellite cells to drive their proliferation before IGF-1Ea (systemic IGF-1) promotes their differentiation. The native peptide has an extremely short half-life (~5 minutes) due to rapid proteolytic degradation. See also PEG-MGF for the pegylated version with extended half-life.

## Status

WADA-prohibited as a peptide hormone. No approved human use.
