## Research Background

Thymosin beta-4 (Tβ4) is an endogenous 43-amino acid protein that sequesters G-actin, promotes actin polymerisation, and plays roles in wound healing, anti-inflammation, and angiogenesis. TB-500, as sold in the grey market, is typically described as the actin-binding domain fragment (residues 17–23), though purity and composition of commercial research products vary widely.

## Human Evidence Status

Tβ4 has been studied in registered clinical trials for dry eye disease and wound healing (RegeneRx). These trials used full-length Tβ4, not the TB-500 fragment. Results have been mixed. The grey-market TB-500 fragment has no completed human RCTs.

## Regulatory Status

WADA prohibits thymosin beta-4 and its fragments as peptide hormones in sport. FDA has not approved any Tβ4-based product for human use.
