## Background

AOD-9604 was developed by Monash University and licensed to Metabolic Pharmaceuticals (Australia). It was claimed that the hGH 176-191 fragment retained hGH's fat-metabolising properties without binding the growth hormone receptor and causing insulin resistance. It received GRAS status as a food ingredient in the US (Commonwealth Scientific recommendation) but failed to demonstrate efficacy in Phase 3 obesity trials.

## Status

Metabolic Pharmaceuticals' Phase 3 programme failed. The compound has no approved drug status anywhere. FDA designated it Category 2 for compounding. It remains available from grey-market vendors with claims that are not supported by clinical evidence.
