## Research Background

Ipamorelin is a synthetic pentapeptide (Aib-His-D-2-Nal-D-Phe-Lys-NH2) developed by Novo Nordisk in the 1990s that potently activates ghrelin/GHS-R1a receptors. Unlike earlier GHRPs (GHRP-2, GHRP-6), ipamorelin is highly selective and does not significantly stimulate cortisol or prolactin. It reached Phase 2 clinical development (for post-operative ileus) before development was discontinued.

## FDA / Regulatory Status

FDA placed ipamorelin on its Category 2 list of bulk substances that may not be used in compounded drug preparations due to safety concerns. It remains widely available from grey-market research chemical vendors.
