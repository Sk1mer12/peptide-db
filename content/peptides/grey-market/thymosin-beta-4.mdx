## Background

Thymosin beta-4 (Tβ4) is the most abundant intracellular actin-sequestering peptide in eukaryotic cells, originally isolated from calf thymus. It is present at high concentrations in platelets and wound fluid, suggesting an important physiological role in tissue repair. Unlike TB-500 (typically described as the actin-binding domain fragment residues 17-23), Tβ4 is the full-length 43-amino acid protein.

## Mechanism

Tβ4 binds G-actin (monomeric) in a 1:1 complex, regulating actin polymerisation dynamics. Beyond cytoskeletal effects, Tβ4 promotes: wound healing via keratinocyte migration; angiogenesis (upregulates VEGF, MMP); anti-apoptotic signalling (activates Akt); anti-inflammatory effects (inhibits NF-κB); and cardiac repair (epicardium activation).

## Clinical Research (RegeneRx)

RegeneRx has conducted Phase 2 trials of Tβ4 for:
- **Dry eye disease** — topical ophthalmic formulation (RGN-259): Phase 3 results mixed
- **Neurotrophic keratopathy** — topical formulation
- **Dermal wound healing** — topical formulation
- **Cardiac repair** — systemic formulation: Phase 2 in cardiac surgery

None have achieved FDA approval.

## Distinction from TB-500

Grey-market "TB-500" typically refers to a fragment peptide, whereas this entry covers full-length Tβ4. The fragment and full-length forms have different commercial availability and may have differing pharmacological profiles.
