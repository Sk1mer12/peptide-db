## Background

Lupus nephritis (LN) affects approximately 50% of SLE patients and is a major cause of morbidity and end-stage kidney disease. Standard of care has historically been mycophenolate mofetil (MMF) plus corticosteroids. Voclosporin adds calcineurin inhibition (via a modified cyclosporine backbone) to provide synergistic T-cell and podocyte protection.

## Mechanism

Like cyclosporine, voclosporin inhibits calcineurin, blocking NFAT-dependent T-cell cytokine production (particularly IL-2). A unique structural modification (a single amino acid side chain alteration) provides more consistent pharmacokinetics without the requirement for blood-level monitoring that complicates cyclosporine use. It also directly stabilises podocyte cytoskeletal architecture in glomeruli.

## AURORA 1 Trial

AURORA 1 demonstrated significantly higher complete renal response rates at 52 weeks (41% vs 23% for MMF alone) with no excess serious adverse events. GFR effects were reversible. A 2-year extension (AURORA 2) confirmed sustained benefit.

## Monitoring

eGFR should be monitored every 2 weeks for 4 weeks after initiation and monthly thereafter. Dose reduction is required if eGFR drops >20% from baseline.
