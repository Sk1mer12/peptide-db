## Mechanism of Action

Glucagon is a 29-amino acid peptide secreted by pancreatic alpha cells in response to hypoglycaemia. It activates glucagon receptors (GCGRs) in hepatocytes, increasing cAMP, promoting glycogenolysis and gluconeogenesis to raise blood glucose. It also relaxes smooth muscle of the GI tract — useful in diagnostic imaging.

## Clinical Indications

- **Severe hypoglycaemia**: emergency treatment in insulin-dependent diabetes
- **Diagnostic aid**: GI hypotonia during radiology
- **Insulinoma localisation**: preoperative
