## Background

Dulaglutide (LY2189265) is a long-acting GLP-1 receptor agonist consisting of two GLP-1 analogue sequences covalently linked to a modified human IgG4 Fc fragment via a small peptide linker. This large fusion protein architecture extends the half-life to approximately 5 days, enabling once-weekly dosing. It was FDA-approved in September 2014.

## Mechanism

Acts on GLP-1 receptors in the pancreas (stimulating glucose-dependent insulin secretion, suppressing glucagon), gut (slowing gastric emptying), and brain (reducing appetite). The IgG4 Fc fusion prevents FcγR binding and complement activation, while the GLP-1 analogue is resistant to DPP-4 degradation.

## Approved Indications

- **Type 2 diabetes mellitus** — to improve glycaemic control
- Cardiovascular risk reduction in adults with T2D with established CVD or multiple CV risk factors

## Cardiovascular Evidence

The REWIND trial (2019) showed that dulaglutide 1.5 mg weekly reduced MACE by 12% vs placebo (HR 0.88, 95% CI 0.79–0.99) in a broad T2D population including those without established CVD — a notable distinction from earlier GLP-1 RA trials.
