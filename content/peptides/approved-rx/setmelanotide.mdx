## Background

Setmelanotide is a cyclic heptapeptide MC4 receptor agonist. It was designed to rescue the downstream melanocortin signalling defect caused by loss-of-function mutations in POMC (pro-opiomelanocortin), PCSK1, or LEPR genes — all of which impair leptin-MC4R pathway signalling and cause severe early-onset obesity with hyperphagia.

## Mechanism

By directly activating MC4R in the hypothalamus, setmelanotide bypasses the upstream genetic defect and restores satiety signalling and energy expenditure regulation. It is selective for MC4R over MC1R (which mediates skin pigmentation), but some MC1R activation causes the characteristic hyperpigmentation side effect.

## Approved Indications

- **POMC, PCSK1, or LEPR deficiency obesity** — adults and children ≥ 6 years (approved November 2020)
- **Bardet-Biedl syndrome** (approved June 2022)
- Requires genetic confirmation of qualifying mutation before use

## Key Distinction from Melanotan-II

Unlike melanotan-II (which non-selectively activates MC1R, MC3R, MC4R, and MC5R), setmelanotide has high MC4R selectivity, which underpins its specific utility in leptin-melanocortin pathway disorders without the full melanocortin spectrum effects.
