## Mechanism of Action

Liraglutide is a fatty-acid-acylated GLP-1 analogue with 97% sequence homology to human GLP-1. Acylation with a C16 fatty acid via a glutamic acid linker enables albumin binding, extending its half-life to approximately 13 hours and permitting once-daily dosing.

## Clinical Indications

- **Type 2 diabetes** (Victoza 1.2–1.8 mg): glycaemic control and cardiovascular risk reduction
- **Obesity** (Saxenda 3.0 mg): chronic weight management
- **Paediatric obesity** (Saxenda): ages 12+ with obesity

## Key Clinical Data

The LEADER trial demonstrated a 13% reduction in major adverse cardiovascular events vs. placebo. The SCALE Obesity trial showed ~8% body weight loss vs. placebo at 56 weeks with the 3 mg dose.
