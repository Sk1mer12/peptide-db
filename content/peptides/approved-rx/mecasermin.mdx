## Background

Primary IGF-1 deficiency (severe) occurs when the IGF-1 gene is defective, or when growth hormone (GH) receptor defects (Laron syndrome) prevent GH from generating IGF-1. In such patients, exogenous GH is ineffective. Mecasermin (Increlex) provides direct IGF-1 replacement.

## Mechanism

IGF-1 binds the IGF-1 receptor (IGF-1R) on growth plate chondrocytes, stimulating longitudinal bone growth via proliferation and differentiation. Unlike GH, it also directly stimulates muscle protein synthesis and has anabolic effects on multiple organ systems.

## Approved Indication

- **Severe primary IGF-1 deficiency** (serum IGF-1 ≤ −3.0 SD) or GH gene deletion with neutralising anti-GH antibodies, in children with open epiphyses
- Defined as failure to respond to GH therapy or its inability to stimulate IGF-1

## Safety Notes

Hypoglycemia is the primary risk — mecasermin must be administered within 20 minutes of a meal or snack. Long-term IGF-1 elevation is associated with theoretical carcinogenic risk (surveillance recommended). Tonsillar hypertrophy requires monitoring for sleep apnoea.
