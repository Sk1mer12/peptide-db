## Background

Carfilzomib is a tetrapeptide epoxyketone (structure: Ac-hFLF-epoxomicin) that irreversibly inhibits the chymotrypsin-like (β5) catalytic subunit of the 20S proteasome. It has greater selectivity for the proteasome than bortezomib and does not inhibit serine proteases, translating to a reduced peripheral neuropathy burden.

## Mechanism

Proteasome inhibition prevents degradation of pro-apoptotic proteins (IκB, p21, p27) and accumulates misfolded proteins in the ER, triggering the unfolded protein response and apoptosis. Multiple myeloma cells are particularly sensitive due to their high immunoglobulin secretory burden and proteasome dependence.

## Approved Indications

- **Relapsed/refractory multiple myeloma** — single agent (after ≥1 prior therapy) and in combinations (KRd, Kd)
- Standard combinations include carfilzomib + lenalidomide + dexamethasone (KRd) and carfilzomib + dexamethasone (Kd)

## Cardiac Monitoring

Cardiovascular events are the most clinically important toxicity. Patients should be evaluated for cardiac function at baseline, and blood pressure monitored during and after infusion. Adequate hydration is required to reduce AKI risk.
