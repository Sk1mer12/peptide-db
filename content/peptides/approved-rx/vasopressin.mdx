## Mechanism of Action

Arginine vasopressin (AVP) acts on three receptor subtypes: V1a receptors (vasoconstriction via Gq/IP3), V2 receptors (antidiuresis via Gs/cAMP/aquaporin-2), and V1b receptors (ACTH release). As a vasopressor, exogenous vasopressin restores vascular tone in norepinephrine-refractory distributive shock states.

## Clinical Indications

- **Vasodilatory shock**: as adjunct to catecholamines (septic shock, post-cardiotomy vasoplegia)
- **Central diabetes insipidus** (historical; desmopressin preferred)
- **Oesophageal variceal bleeding** (historical)
