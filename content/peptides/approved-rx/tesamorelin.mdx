## Mechanism of Action

Tesamorelin is a synthetic analogue of endogenous growth hormone-releasing hormone (GHRH). It binds GHRH receptors in the pituitary gland, stimulating pulsatile GH secretion, which in turn increases IGF-1 levels and promotes lipolysis of visceral adipose tissue.

## Clinical Indications

- **HIV-associated lipodystrophy**: reduction of excess visceral abdominal fat in adults

## Notes

Tesamorelin is banned in sport (WADA) due to its GH-stimulating properties. Off-label use in healthy individuals to improve body composition is not supported by approved labelling, and the compound's effects on IGF-1 raise concerns about long-term cancer risk with chronic use outside approved contexts.
