## Background

Cholecystokinin (CCK) is an endogenous gut peptide hormone released by I-cells of the duodenum and jejunum in response to fat and protein in the lumen. It stimulates gallbladder contraction, relaxes the sphincter of Oddi, and stimulates pancreatic enzyme secretion. Sincalide is the synthetic C-terminal octapeptide of CCK, containing the biologically essential sulfated tyrosine residue.

## Diagnostic Applications

**Cholescintigraphy (HIDA scan):** IV sincalide is administered to measure gallbladder ejection fraction (GBEF). A GBEF below 35–38% is considered abnormal and consistent with biliary dyskinesia or functional gallbladder disorder.

**Pancreatic function testing:** Sincalide stimulates pancreatic exocrine secretion, enabling quantification of lipase, amylase, and other enzyme output for diagnosis of exocrine pancreatic insufficiency.

**Pancreatic/biliary imaging:** Can be combined with secretin for combined pancreatic stimulation.

## Dosing

For cholescintigraphy: 0.02 µg/kg IV over 3 minutes (more physiologic response) or 60 minutes (preferred by most guidelines for accurate GBEF measurement).
