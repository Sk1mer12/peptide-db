## Mechanism of Action

Semaglutide is a glucagon-like peptide-1 (GLP-1) receptor agonist. It mimics endogenous GLP-1, stimulating insulin secretion in a glucose-dependent manner, suppressing glucagon, slowing gastric emptying, and reducing appetite via central hypothalamic pathways.

## Clinical Indications

- **Type 2 diabetes** (Ozempic, Rybelsus): glycaemic control
- **Chronic weight management** (Wegovy): adults with BMI ≥30 or ≥27 with weight-related comorbidity
- **Cardiovascular risk reduction** (Ozempic): reduces major adverse cardiovascular events in T2DM patients

## Key Clinical Data

The SUSTAIN and STEP trial programmes demonstrated HbA1c reductions of 1.5–2.0% and weight loss of 10–15% body weight over 68 weeks at the 2.4 mg dose (Wegovy). The SELECT trial (2023) showed 20% reduction in MACE in people with obesity without diabetes.
