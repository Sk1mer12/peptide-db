## Background

Secondary hyperparathyroidism (SHPT) is nearly universal in end-stage renal disease patients on hemodialysis and is driven by elevated phosphate, low calcitriol, and impaired calcium sensing. Elevated PTH promotes renal osteodystrophy and cardiovascular calcification. Etelcalcetide is a novel D-amino acid heptapeptide calcimimetic that, unlike cinacalcet (small molecule), can be given IV at the dialysis machine.

## Mechanism

Etelcalcetide forms a disulphide bond with cysteine 482 on the calcium-sensing receptor (CaSR) extracellular domain. This allosteric activation sensitises the CaSR to calcium, reducing parathyroid gland PTH secretion even at lower serum calcium concentrations.

## Approved Indication

- **Secondary hyperparathyroidism** in adults with chronic kidney disease on hemodialysis
- Administered IV (5 mg initial dose) three times weekly at the end of each dialysis session

## Advantage Over Cinacalcet

IV administration eliminates adherence issues associated with oral cinacalcet and has demonstrated superior PTH reduction in head-to-head trials, with similar rates of hypocalcemia.
