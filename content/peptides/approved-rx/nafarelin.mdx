## Background

Nafarelin is a synthetic GnRH analogue substituting D-Nal(2) at position 6 and a Pro-NH-Et amide at position 10, resulting in greater receptor binding affinity and resistance to enzymatic degradation compared with native GnRH. FDA approved in 1990, Synarel was among the first intranasal GnRH agonists available.

## Mechanism

Pulsatile GnRH drives gonadotropin release, whereas continuous stimulation with nafarelin produces initial flare followed by receptor downregulation, progressive desensitization, and suppression of LH, FSH, estrogen, and testosterone to castrate or prepubertal levels within 4 weeks.

## Approved Indications

- **Endometriosis** — management of pain and reduction of endometriotic lesions (up to 6 months)
- **Central precocious puberty** — children ≥ 2 years with GnRH-dependent CPP

## Dosing Notes

Endometriosis: 200 µg (one spray) in one nostril in the morning and the other nostril in the evening. CPP: 1600 µg/day (8 sprays, alternating nostrils). Bone density loss is a concern with courses exceeding 6 months; add-back hormone therapy is recommended for longer use.
