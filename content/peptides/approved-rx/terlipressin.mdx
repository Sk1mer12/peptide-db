## Background

Hepatorenal syndrome type 1 (HRS-1) is a form of acute kidney injury occurring in advanced cirrhosis characterised by severe splanchnic vasodilation and compensatory renal vasoconstriction. Terlipressin, a synthetic triglycyl-lysine vasopressin prodrug, acts as a potent vasoconstrictor to reverse the splanchnic vasodilation driving the syndrome.

Terlipressin had been used in Europe (Glypressin) for decades before FDA approval in August 2022, following the CONFIRM trial.

## Mechanism

After slow enzymatic cleavage of the triglycine moiety, terlipressin releases lysine vasopressin, which activates V1 receptors in splanchnic vasculature, producing prolonged vasoconstriction. This reduces portal hypertension and redistributes blood flow to improve renal perfusion.

## CONFIRM Trial

The CONFIRM trial demonstrated that terlipressin significantly improved reversal of HRS-1 (confirmed HRS reversal 32% vs 17% placebo, p=0.006). However, it also showed excess serious adverse events including respiratory failure, particularly in patients with ACLF grade 3, leading to a black box warning.

## Safety Concerns

FDA added a black box warning: terlipressin should not be used in patients with hypoxia (SpO2 < 90%) and requires close monitoring for respiratory deterioration. Its benefit is most clearly established in HRS-1 without pre-existing respiratory compromise.
