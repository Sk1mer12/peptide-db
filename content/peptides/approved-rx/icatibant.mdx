## Background

Hereditary angioedema (HAE) is caused by C1-esterase inhibitor deficiency, leading to uncontrolled kallikrein activation and bradykinin overproduction. Bradykinin acting on B2 receptors drives vascular permeability and the characteristic swelling attacks, which can be life-threatening when involving the larynx.

Icatibant is a synthetic decapeptide with five non-natural amino acids, designed as a potent, selective, and competitive bradykinin B2 receptor antagonist. FDA approved August 2011.

## Mechanism

Icatibant competitively blocks the bradykinin B2 receptor, preventing bradykinin from inducing vasodilation and increased vascular permeability. It does not affect the underlying C1-inhibitor deficiency but rapidly terminates the bradykinin-mediated inflammatory cascade.

## Clinical Efficacy

In the FAST-3 trial, icatibant significantly reduced time to symptom relief vs placebo (median 2.0 vs 19.8 hours). It is indicated for on-demand self-administration (30 mg) for all attack types (abdominal, cutaneous, laryngeal).

## Administration

Patients or caregivers can be trained to self-administer. A second dose may be given after 6 hours if insufficient response; a third dose after a further 6 hours (maximum 3 doses in 24 hours).
