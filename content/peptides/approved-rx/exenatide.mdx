## Background

Exenatide is a synthetic version of exendin-4, a 39-amino acid peptide originally isolated from the venom of the Gila monster (*Heloderma suspectum*). It shares ~53% sequence homology with human GLP-1 and acts as a potent, full GLP-1 receptor agonist. It was the first GLP-1 receptor agonist to receive FDA approval (April 2005, Byetta).

## Mechanism

Exenatide stimulates insulin secretion in a glucose-dependent manner, suppresses inappropriately elevated glucagon, slows gastric emptying, and reduces appetite via central GLP-1 receptor signalling. Unlike endogenous GLP-1, exenatide is resistant to DPP-4 degradation due to its N-terminal Gly-Glu substitution.

## Approved Indications

- **Type 2 diabetes mellitus** — adjunct to diet and exercise
- Available as twice-daily (Byetta 5 µg / 10 µg) and once-weekly extended-release (Bydureon 2 mg)

## Cardiovascular Evidence

The EXSCEL trial (2017) showed a non-significant trend toward cardiovascular benefit; exenatide weekly did not significantly reduce MACE vs placebo but was non-inferior. Less robust CV evidence than semaglutide or liraglutide.
