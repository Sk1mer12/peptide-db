## Mechanism of Action

Desmopressin is a structural analogue of arginine vasopressin with D-arginine substituted for L-arginine and a deaminated cysteine at position 1. These modifications increase antidiuretic potency and duration while nearly eliminating vasopressor activity. It acts at V2 receptors in renal collecting ducts, promoting water reabsorption by inserting aquaporin-2 channels.

## Clinical Indications

- **Central diabetes insipidus**: antidiuretic therapy
- **Primary nocturnal enuresis**: in children
- **Nocturia in adults**: (Nocdurna)
- **Haemostasis**: mild haemophilia A and von Willebrand disease type 1 (raises vWF and factor VIII)
