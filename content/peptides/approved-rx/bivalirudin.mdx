## Background

Bivalirudin is a synthetic analogue of hirudin — the direct thrombin inhibitor from the medicinal leech *Hirudo medicinalis*. As a 20-amino acid peptide, it bivalently inhibits thrombin by simultaneously binding both the active catalytic site and the substrate recognition exosite-1, producing a more complete inhibition than univalent direct thrombin inhibitors.

## Mechanism

Bivalirudin's C-terminal segment (D-Phe-Pro-Arg-Pro) occupies the thrombin active site and inhibits fibrinogen cleavage. Its N-terminal hirudin-like sequence binds thrombin exosite-1. The active site–bound moiety is slowly cleaved by thrombin itself, providing intrinsic reversibility and short half-life without antidote requirements.

## Approved Indications

- **Percutaneous coronary intervention (PCI)** — as anticoagulant with provisional use of GPI
- **Heparin-induced thrombocytopenia (HIT)** and HIT with thrombosis — as anticoagulation
- Unstable angina undergoing PTCA

## Advantages Over Heparin

Predictable, weight-based dosing without anti-factor Xa monitoring; does not bind plasma proteins or platelet factor 4 (no HIT risk); renally cleared active drug eliminates AT-III dependence. HORIZONS-AMI demonstrated reduced major bleeding with bivalirudin vs heparin + GPI in STEMI.
