## Mechanism of Action

Tirzepatide is a synthetic peptide that acts as a dual agonist at both GIP (glucose-dependent insulinotropic polypeptide) and GLP-1 receptors. This dual action provides complementary metabolic effects: GIP receptor activation enhances insulin secretion and may improve insulin sensitivity in adipose tissue, while GLP-1 receptor activation suppresses appetite and slows gastric emptying.

## Clinical Indications

- **Type 2 diabetes** (Mounjaro): glycaemic control in adults
- **Chronic weight management** (Zepbound): adults with BMI ≥30 or ≥27 with comorbidity

## Key Clinical Data

The SURMOUNT-1 trial demonstrated up to 22.5% body weight reduction at the 15 mg dose — the largest weight loss seen in a pharmacological obesity trial at the time of publication. The SURPASS programme showed superior HbA1c and weight reduction vs. semaglutide 1 mg in SURPASS-2.
