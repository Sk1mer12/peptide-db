## Background

Amylin is a 37-amino acid peptide hormone co-secreted with insulin from pancreatic β-cells in response to meals. It is deficient in type 1 diabetes and relatively deficient in type 2. Pramlintide is an analogue in which alanine replaces prolines at positions 25, 28, and 29 to prevent the fibril formation that occurs with native amylin — the same aggregation that causes islet amyloid deposits in type 2 diabetes.

## Mechanism

Pramlintide slows gastric emptying, suppresses postprandial glucagon secretion, and reduces food intake via central amylin receptor signalling. It does not independently cause hypoglycemia but substantially increases hypoglycemia risk when used with mealtime insulin (black box warning).

## Approved Indications

- **Type 1 diabetes** — adjunct to mealtime insulin (starting dose 15 µg)
- **Type 2 diabetes** — adjunct to mealtime insulin ± oral agents (starting dose 60 µg)

## Clinical Notes

Pramlintide typically produces modest HbA1c reductions (0.3–0.6%) and 1–2 kg weight loss when added to insulin. It requires a 50% reduction in mealtime insulin dose at initiation to mitigate severe hypoglycemia risk.
