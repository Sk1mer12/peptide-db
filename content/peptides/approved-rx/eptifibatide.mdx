## Background

Eptifibatide is a cyclic heptapeptide (containing the KGD — Lys-Gly-Asp — sequence) derived from barbourin, a disintegrin found in the venom of the southeastern pygmy rattlesnake (*Sistrurus miliarius barbouri*). The KGD sequence selectively inhibits the αIIbβ3 integrin (GPIIb/IIIa) with high specificity over other RGD-binding integrins.

## Mechanism

GPIIb/IIIa (αIIbβ3) is the most abundant platelet surface receptor. Upon platelet activation, conformational change exposes the fibrinogen-binding site. Eptifibatide competitively blocks fibrinogen, von Willebrand factor, and other ligands from binding GPIIb/IIIa, effectively preventing platelet cross-linking and thrombus formation.

## Approved Indications

- **Acute coronary syndrome (ACS)** — NSTEMI/UA managed medically or with PCI
- **Elective PCI** — as antiplatelet adjunct during stenting

## Clinical Evidence

PURSUIT (1998) showed 15% relative risk reduction in death/MI with eptifibatide in ACS. ESPRIT (2000) demonstrated significant reduction in composite endpoint during stenting. Acute profound thrombocytopenia (platelet count below 20,000/µL) can occur within hours — platelet count monitoring required.
