## Background

Histrelin acetate is a synthetic nonapeptide analogue of GnRH with approximately 200 times the potency of native GnRH. Unlike native GnRH which is released in pulses, continuous exposure from the subcutaneous implant causes downregulation of pituitary GnRH receptors and suppression of LH and FSH secretion, leading to castrate levels of sex steroids within 4 weeks.

## Delivery System

The histrelin implant (Supprelin LA: 50 mg; Vantas: 50 mg) is a non-biodegradable hydrogel reservoir inserted subcutaneously in the inner upper arm. It releases approximately 65 µg/day continuously for 12 months, then requires replacement.

## Approved Indications

- **Central precocious puberty (Supprelin LA)** — children with onset of secondary sex characteristics before age 8 (girls) or 9 (boys)
- **Advanced prostate cancer (Vantas)** — palliative androgen deprivation therapy

## Initial Flare

As with all GnRH agonists, histrelin causes a brief testosterone/estrogen surge in the first 1–2 weeks before receptor downregulation suppresses gonadal steroidogenesis.
