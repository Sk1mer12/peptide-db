## Background

Pegcetacoplan is a PEGylated peptide targeting complement component C3 — the central convergence point of all three complement activation pathways (classical, lectin, and alternative). By inhibiting C3, it prevents both intra- and extravascular haemolysis in paroxysmal nocturnal haemoglobinuria (PNH) and complement-mediated photoreceptor/RPE damage in geographic atrophy (GA) from AMD.

## Mechanism

The cyclic peptide moiety (APL-1) binds C3 and C3b, preventing their cleavage by C3 convertase and blocking all downstream complement activation including C5 cleavage, MAC formation, and opsonisation. PEGylation extends the half-life and reduces immunogenicity.

## Approved Indications

- **Paroxysmal nocturnal haemoglobinuria (PNH)** — Empaveli (1080 mg SC twice weekly); superior to eculizumab in PRINCE trial with higher haemoglobin response
- **Geographic atrophy secondary to AMD** — Syfovre (15 mg intravitreal monthly or every other month); first FDA-approved treatment for GA (February 2023)

## Comparison with Eculizumab

Prior PNH standard-of-care eculizumab targets C5 (downstream of C3). Targeting C3 allows control of both intravascular AND extravascular haemolysis — a key advantage, as ~25–30% of eculizumab patients experience persistent extravascular haemolysis due to C3b-mediated RBC opsonisation.
