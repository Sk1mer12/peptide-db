## Background

Achondroplasia is caused by a gain-of-function mutation in FGFR3, which constitutively inhibits the chondrocyte proliferation and differentiation required for endochondral bone growth. CNP normally activates NPR-B receptors on chondrocytes to stimulate cGMP production, counteracting FGFR3 signalling. However, native CNP has a half-life of only ~2 minutes.

Vosoritide (BMN 111) is an engineered CNP analogue with a 37-amino acid extension that confers resistance to neutral endopeptidase degradation and extends the half-life to ~28 minutes, enabling once-daily subcutaneous administration.

## Mechanism

Vosoritide activates NPR-B receptors in the growth plate, increasing intracellular cGMP, which suppresses MAP kinase signalling downstream of FGFR3. This partially restores normal chondrocyte proliferation and differentiation.

## Clinical Evidence

The pivotal Phase 3 trial (Savarirayan et al, NEJM 2020) showed a mean increase in annualised growth velocity of ~1.57 cm/year vs placebo over 52 weeks. Open-label extension data suggest continued growth velocity increases maintained over 3–4 years of treatment.

## Limitations

Vosoritide increases height velocity but does not normalize final adult height. Long-term studies are ongoing. It is indicated only while growth plates remain open.
