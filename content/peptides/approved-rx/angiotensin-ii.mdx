## Mechanism of Action

Angiotensin II is an octapeptide that acts on AT1 receptors in vascular smooth muscle, causing vasoconstriction, and on the adrenal gland, stimulating aldosterone release and sodium/water retention. As a vasopressor, it is complementary to catecholamines because it exploits a different receptor system.

## Clinical Indications

- **Vasodilatory/distributive shock**: adjunct to catecholamines when MAP targets are not met; particularly in patients with high norepinephrine requirements
