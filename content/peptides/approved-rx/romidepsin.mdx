## Background

Romidepsin (FK228) is a naturally occurring bicyclic depsipeptide originally isolated from *Chromobacterium violaceum* soil bacteria in Japan. It was FDA-approved in 2009 for cutaneous T-cell lymphoma (CTCL) and in 2011 for peripheral T-cell lymphoma (PTCL), making it the first natural-product HDAC inhibitor approved for oncology.

## Mechanism

Romidepsin is a prodrug. After cellular uptake, glutathione reduces the disulphide bond, releasing the active thiol form which chelates zinc ions in the catalytic site of class I and class II histone deacetylases (HDACs). This prevents histone deacetylation, leading to chromatin remodelling, altered gene expression, cell cycle arrest at G1/S and G2/M boundaries, and apoptosis — primarily in rapidly dividing cancer cells.

## Approved Indications

- **Cutaneous T-cell lymphoma** (CTCL) — after at least 1 prior therapy (FDA 2009)
- **Peripheral T-cell lymphoma** (PTCL) — after at least 1 prior therapy (FDA 2011)

## Dosing

14 mg/m² IV over 4 hours on Days 1, 8, and 15 of a 28-day cycle.
