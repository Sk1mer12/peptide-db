## Mechanism of Action

Cagrilintide is a fatty-acid-acylated amylin analogue. Amylin receptors (AMY1R, AMY3R) are activated in the area postrema, hypothalamus, and nucleus tractus solitarius, where amylin coordinates meal termination signals, reduces glucagon secretion, and slows gastric emptying. Cagrilintide's half-life of ~7 days enables once-weekly dosing.

## CagriSema Combination

The combination of cagrilintide 2.4 mg + semaglutide 2.4 mg (CagriSema) is in Phase 3 trials (REDEFINE programme). Phase 1/2 data showed ~15.6% weight loss at 32 weeks vs. ~8.0% for semaglutide alone.

## Status

Not FDA-approved. Phase 3 data expected 2025–2026. Regulatory submission timeline is dependent on REDEFINE programme outcomes.
